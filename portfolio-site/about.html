About Denzell Richardson

I'm a Computer Science student at Borough of Manhattan Community College (BMCC) who loves building clean, responsive web apps and solving real problems with code. From Brooklyn, NY, I get excited digging into how things work—whether it’s in the browser or under the hood.

The Story

It started with curiosity. I would look at websites and wonder how they were made. That curiosity pulled me into hands-on learning: writing my first HTML, then styling with CSS, then wrestling with JavaScript and the console.

I care about more than syntax—I want to know why something is structured a certain way, why responsive design matters, and why teams rely on Git to keep their work in sync. Asking those questions makes me a better developer.

I read documentation, tinker in the terminal, and don’t mind breaking things if it means I get to fix them better. I solve problems step by step, work well with others, and bring focus and energy to anything I build.

My Approach

Precision — Code isn't just functional; it needs to be clean, maintainable, and well-documented.
Curiosity — I actively seek out new technologies and best practices. Continuous learning isn't optional—it's essential.
Collaboration — Great products come from great teams. I communicate clearly, ask thoughtful questions, and value feedback.
Ownership — When I build something, I own it. I debug it thoroughly, optimize it, and take pride in the result.
